Namibia's Geological Setting

Namibia's uranium deposits are primarily located in the Namib Desert and surrounding regions, characterized by:

  • Ancient Precambrian basement rocks
  • Karoo-aged sedimentary basins
  • Desert environment with unique weathering
  • Calcrete and caliche formations

Processing Stages

Stage 1: Calcrete Processing

Namibian calcrete ores require specialized crushing and grinding to liberate uranium from calcium carbonate matrix.

Stage 2: Acid Leaching

Calcrete-hosted uranium requires sulfuric acid leaching under specific temperature and pH conditions.

Stage 3: Ion Exchange

Uranium extraction using resin columns, optimized for Namibia's low-grade calcrete ores.

Stage 4: Yellowcake Production

Final precipitation and drying produces uranium concentrate for export to global markets.
